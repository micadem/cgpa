THIS IS HOW TO CALCULATE YOUR CGPA

This article aims to help you understand the grading system of the University of Ibadan.

The university of Ibadan operates a 4-point grading system which is calculated based on one's performance in each course. This therefore makes it necessary for students to work hard and be focused on their studies.

    All the courses you're to register for as a student of the university can be classified into three categories which are:

  • Elective
  • Compulsory
  • Required courses
Forms of Courses
Compulsory Courses: Courses specified by a department, which a student must take and PASS.

Required Courses Specified courses within a department which a student must take but not have to necessarily pass.

Elective Courses Courses from which a student can/has to choose from in order to make up the required additional units for the award of degree.

Strive hard to pass all courses as they all have the same effect as to their Units on your GP. At the end of first year, a student who passed less than 24 units will be asked to withdraw from the university. Also, the CGPA at the end of each session should not be less than 1.0 else you'll be force to withdraw from the university.
Here are some terms used in the grading system you need to know as a student
GPA is Grade Point Average and it's the Average of your Grade Point in a particular semester not necessarily the first, second, fifth or last semester. It could be any.
CGPA* is Cumulative Grade Point Average and it's the Total Average of your Grade Point till your present semester/level.
At the end of first year, a student who passed less than 24 units will be asked to withdraw from the university. Also, the CGPA at the end of each session should not be less than 1.0 else you'll be force to withdraw from the university.```
CGPA range under the 4-point grading scale for classification of degrees:

3.5 – 4.00 First Class Honours
3.0 – 3.49 Second Class Honours (Upper Division)
2.0 – 2.99. Second Class Honours (Lower Division)
1.0 – 1.99 Third Class

If you have a minimum score of 70 in all courses in a semester, you'll have a perfect GP for that and if you have nothing less than 70 in all your courses throughout your stay in the university you'll graduate with a perfect CGPA, a handful number of students have done that, you can also do it

HOW TO CALCULATE GPA

Let's say Tabitha Dorcas is a fresh student in the department of economics and offers the following courses in First Semester ECO102, ECO103, GES107, GES108, POS112, SOC103, SOC104 and SOC105.
Dorcas has the following scores in
ECO102 — 63
ECO103 — 68
GES107 — 82
GES108 — 88
POS112 — 63
SOC103 — 64
SOC104 — 76
SOC105 — 70
COURSE UNIT GRADE POINT WEIGHED GRADE POINT
ECO102 3 3 9
ECO 103 3 3 9
GES 107 2 4 8
GES 108 2 4 8
POS 112 3 3 9
SOC 103 3 3 9
SOC 104 3 4 12
SOC 105 3 4 12
22 76
GPA = sum of weighed grade points/ total units = 76/22 = 3.45454545
GPA= 3.45 (3SF)
So, Dorcas is in Second Class (Upper Division)
